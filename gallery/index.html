PCB Design to Fab in 8 Steps

From schematic to finished board: the complete PCB workflow.

Full Caption
Ever wondered what happens between your schematic and a finished PCB?

Here's the complete 8 step workflow, from concept to quality tested board.

1. Requirements: Define specs with your full team.
2. Schematic: Capture electrical connections as your blueprint.
3. Placement: Position components for signal flow and thermals.
4. Routing: Trace paths, copper pours, impedance matching.
5. Verification: DRC and DFM checks catch errors before fab.
6. File Generation: Gerbers, drill files, BOM, all production data.
7. Fabrication: Etching, lamination, drilling, plating, finishing.
8. Quality: AOI, electrical testing, visual inspection.

Skipping any of these steps costs you time and money downstream.

At Cu Circuits, every board goes through this full pipeline with rigorous DFM review built in.

6 Common 3D Printing Mistakes and How to Fix Them

Your 3D prints keep failing and you cannot figure out why?

It is almost always one of these six mistakes.

We run 3D printers daily at Cu Circuits for prototyping enclosures and jigs. After thousands of prints, we have seen every failure mode. And the fix is usually simpler than you think.

1. Skipping bed leveling (the #1 cause of first layer failures)
2. Wrong temperature for your material
3. Walls too thin to actually print solid
4. Printing overhangs without supports
5. Using filament that absorbed moisture
6. Printing too fast for your setup
